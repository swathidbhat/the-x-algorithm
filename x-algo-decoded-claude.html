Part 1 — The Consumer's View

How does X decide what to show you?

Think of your For You feed like a restaurant kitchen. Before a dish (tweet) reaches your plate, it goes through multiple stages: sourcing ingredients, checking quality, cooking & plating, and final taste-testing.

Step 1: Gather Candidates

The algorithm collects around 1,500+ candidate tweets from 8 different sources, all at once. About half come from people you follow (in-network), and the other half from people you don't follow (out-of-network). README.md

Step 2: Score Every Tweet

A neural network (called the "Heavy Ranker", served via Navi in Rust) looks at each tweet and predicts how likely you specifically are to interact with it — like it, reply, share, watch a video, etc. It checks 17 different engagement types. NaviModelScorer.scala

Step 3: Filter & Mix

After scoring, 25+ filters remove tweets that are spam, from blocked accounts, that you've already seen, or that violate safety rules. Then the top ~50 surviving tweets are mixed with ads and suggestions to form your feed. visibilitylib/ & home-mixer/filter/

Part 2 — The Ranking Engine

What does the algorithm prioritise?

Every tweet gets a single score. That score is a mix of 17 predictions about your behaviour, each weighted differently. Think of it like a judge scoring a gymnastics routine across multiple categories.

The Scoring Formula

In plain English: for each engagement type, multiply how likely you are to do it by how much X values it, then add them all up.

Score = (P(like) × wlike) + (P(retweet) × wretweet) + (P(reply) × wreply) + … − (P(report) × wreport)

The report weight can go as low as −20,000 — far heavier than any other negative signal (others cap at −1,000). A tweet people might report gets brutally punished. HomeGlobalParams.scala → ModelWeights.ReportParam

What This Means For You

The algorithm strongly favours tweets from people you follow (out-of-network gets a 25% penalty), favours original posts over replies (replies get a 25% penalty), and diversifies your feed so one person doesn't flood it. If you tap "Show More Like This", similar content gets a massive 20× boost. Tap "Show Less", and it gets crushed to 5% of its score.

Part 3 — The Creator's View

How does the algorithm distribute your content?

When you post a tweet, it doesn't just go to your followers. It can spread outward in expanding rings — but only if it earns its way there through genuine engagement.

🔔 Push Notifications (strictest quality bar)
🧠 Interest Matching — SimClusters (needs 9+ likes)
👥 Friends-of-Followers — UTEG Social Proof
👥 Followers
💬

Ring 1: Your Followers (Automatic)

Your tweet immediately enters the candidate pool for every person who follows you. But "entering the pool" doesn't mean they'll see it — it competes with up to 600 other tweets in their feed. A light ranking model in the search index (Earlybird) gives it an initial relevance score. ScoredTweetsParam.scala → InNetworkMaxTweetsToFetchParam = 600

Ring 2: Friends of Followers (Earned via Likes)

When your followers like or retweet your tweet, something powerful happens: the UTEG (User-Tweet-Entity-Graph) makes your tweet visible to their followers — people who don't follow you at all. This is the "𝕏 liked" distribution you see in your feed. The more of your followers who engage, the wider this ring expands. cr-mixer/ → UtegTweetCandidateGenerator

Ring 3: Interest-Matched Strangers (Earned via Topic Relevance)

Once your tweet gets 9 or more likes, it gets processed by SimClusters — a system that maps every tweet and every user into 145,000 interest clusters. If your tweet matches someone's interest profile, it can appear in their feed even with zero social connection to you. simclusters-ann/ & SimClustersInterestedInCandidateGeneration

Ring 4: Push Notifications (Hardest to Reach)

The notification pipeline (pushservice) has its own, much stricter quality bar. It runs separate ML models to predict both whether someone will open the notification AND engage with your content. It applies extra health and quality filters that the regular timeline doesn't. Only the highest-quality out-of-network content makes it here. pushservice/ → PushMLModelScorer

How Long Does Distribution Last?

Your tweet's distribution window is shaped by several time-based mechanisms:

  • ⏰ Cache TTL: Scored tweets are cached for just 3 minutes before re-evaluation CachedScoredTweets.TTLParam = 3.minutes
  • 👁 Impression filter: Once someone sees your tweet, it's recorded and excluded from their future feeds
  • 🕐 Age filter: Tweets beyond a configurable age threshold are removed from candidacy
  • 🌱 Evergreen rescue: A dedicated pipeline can resurface high-quality older content that remains relevant EvergreenDeepRetrievalListwiseRescoringProvider
Part 4 — The Creator's Playbook

What kind of content should you create?

Here's what the algorithm's code actually reveals about what performs well. Every tip below is backed by a specific parameter, feature, or pipeline in the codebase.

Original Posts Beat Replies

Original tweet 1.0× Full score
Reply 0.75× 25% penalty

Replies get a built-in 25% score reduction. Share your key ideas as standalone posts, not replies. ReplyScaleFactorParam = 0.75

Fewer, Better Posts > Flooding the Timeline

Every additional tweet the algorithm shows from you to the same person scores progressively lower:

1.00× 1st post
0.63× 2nd post
0.44× 3rd post
0.34× 4th post
0.25× 5th+

Author diversity decay: factor = 0.5, floor = 0.25. Your 5th tweet barely reaches 25% of its original score. AuthorDiversityDecayFactor = 0.5, AuthorDiversityFloor = 0.25

The Do's and Don'ts Cheat Sheet

Do

Earn Likes From Your Followers First

Likes from followers trigger UTEG distribution to their network. SimClusters requires 9+ likes to even activate interest-matching. Your followers are the launchpad. MinFavToHydrate

Do

Reply to Your Own Replies

The algorithm has a dedicated prediction for "will the author reply back?" (PredictedReplyEngagedByAuthorScore). Being a conversational creator is an explicit positive signal.

Do

Make Videos People Finish

The algorithm tracks 50%+ video completion as a distinct positive signal. Front-load the hook. Shorter, compelling videos that people actually watch beat long ones they skip. PredictedVideoPlayback50ScoreFeature

Do

Write Content People Dwell On

15+ seconds spent reading your tweet detail, or 20+ seconds on your profile, are both explicit positive signals with their own prediction models. Make it worth stopping for. PredictedTweetDetailDwellScore (15s) · PredictedProfileDwelledScore (20s)

Do

Stay On-Topic (Clearly)

SimClusters maps tweets into 145K interest clusters. Clear, specific topics get stronger cluster matches, meaning better interest-based distribution. Vague, unfocused content is harder to recommend.

Do

Create Content People Bookmark & Share

Both bookmarks and shares have dedicated prediction signals with full positive weight ranges. These are high-intent signals the algorithm values. PredictedBookmarkScoreFeature · PredictedShareScoreFeature

Don't

Post Too Frequently

Author diversity decay multiplies down at 0.5× per extra tweet shown, flooring at 0.25×. Fewer high-quality posts outperform a flood of mediocre ones.

Don't

Post Repetitive Visual Content

Media cluster decay and CLIP image cluster decay penalise visually similar content shown to the same user. Vary your visual style. ImpressedMediaClusterBasedListwiseRescoringProvider · ImpressedImageClusterBasedListwiseRescoringProvider

Don't

Trigger Reports

The report prediction weight can go to −20,000 — the heaviest negative signal in the entire system. Even strong negative feedback caps at −1,000. Avoid controversial or low-quality content that people might report. ModelWeights.ReportParam: max = 0, min = -20,000

Don't

Use Clickbait Without Follow-Through

The algorithm distinguishes "good clicks" (clicks followed by likes/replies) from empty clicks. PredictedGoodClickScore means clicked AND then engaged. A click with no follow-up doesn't help.

💡 The Biggest Lever: "Show More / Show Less"

When any user taps "Show More Like This" on content similar to yours, similar content gets a 20× score multiplier. When they tap "Show Less", similar content drops to 5% of its score. The similarity threshold is 0.67 (embedding cosine similarity). Creating content that people actively want more of is the single most powerful amplifier in the algorithm.
